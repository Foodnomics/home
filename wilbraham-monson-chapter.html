Food Waste Trends

Data Analysis

Wilbraham Monson Academy's Foodnomics chapter, led by Jiayi Zhou, has achieved excellent results in reducing food waste across both lunch and dinner services. Jiayi's systematic approach to data collection and student engagement has created a strong foundation for environmental responsibility at the academy.

Starting in September 2024, Wilbraham Monson Academy recorded significant food waste levels with lunch averaging $373.85 and dinner averaging $398.85. Through persistent awareness campaigns and strategic use of the Foodnomics app, Jiayi has led her school to impressive reductions across the full academic year.

Outstanding achievements at Wilbraham Monson Academy include:

  1. Significant lunch improvements: 45.8% reduction from September ($373.85) to May ($202.65)
  2. Strong dinner progress: 45.0% reduction from September ($398.85) to May ($219.50)
  3. Consistent reductions: Achieved steady decreases across most months
  4. Remarkable cost savings: Combined monthly reduction of over $350 in food waste
  5. Sustained leadership: Maintained engagement throughout the full academic year

Jiayi Zhou's leadership at Wilbraham Monson Academy demonstrates exceptional dedication to environmental sustainability. Her nearly 45% average reduction across both lunch and dinner services represents one of the strongest performances in the Foodnomics network, showing how consistent student leadership can create lasting institutional change.

Foodnomics

At Foodnomics, we recognize a long-standing problem as poverty and hunger, and we are determined to address it with the next generation of the world's leaders. The world produces more than enough food to feed its people, yet millions of people are still hungry, struggling to find their next meal. A contributing factor to this problem is waste. All across America, 60 million tons of food are being discarded each year, This is especially prevalent in dining halls across the country. But together, we can lessen this problem. By founding a chapter at your school and using the resources that we provide, you can collect data on how much food is being wasted and its monetary value, then work with your dining hall staff to take appropriate measures to fix the problem by saving both money and resources. By influencing your immediate community, you are helping your locality, but collectively we can take on the world's most important problem, one meal at a time.
