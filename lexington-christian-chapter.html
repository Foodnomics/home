Food Waste Trends

Data Analysis

Lexington Christian Academy's Foodnomics chapter, led by Zhefu (Jeff) Lei, has demonstrated exceptional results in reducing food waste across both lunch and dinner periods. Jeff's comprehensive approach to tracking both meals has provided valuable insights into campus-wide consumption patterns.

Starting in September 2024 with lunch waste averaging $481.25 and dinner waste at $464.00, Jeff implemented a systematic strategy using the Foodnomics app to engage students in waste reduction efforts across all dining periods.

Outstanding achievements at Lexington Christian Academy include:

  1. Significant lunch improvements: 28.8% reduction from September ($481.25) to May ($342.40)
  2. Consistent dinner progress: 26.0% reduction from September ($464.00) to May ($343.20)
  3. Comprehensive tracking: Successfully monitored waste patterns across both meal periods
  4. Sustained reductions: Achieved consistent month-over-month improvements
  5. Total monthly savings: Combined reduction of over $250 in monthly food waste

Jeff Lei's leadership in implementing comprehensive waste tracking and educating peers about mindful consumption has created a sustainable culture of environmental responsibility at Lexington Christian Academy. The chapter demonstrates how systematic data collection across multiple meal periods can yield significant environmental and economic benefits.

Foodnomics

At Foodnomics, we recognize a long-standing problem as poverty and hunger, and we are determined to address it with the next generation of the world's leaders. The world produces more than enough food to feed its people, yet millions of people are still hungry, struggling to find their next meal. A contributing factor to this problem is waste. All across America, 60 million tons of food are being discarded each year, This is especially prevalent in dining halls across the country. But together, we can lessen this problem. By founding a chapter at your school and using the resources that we provide, you can collect data on how much food is being wasted and its monetary value, then work with your dining hall staff to take appropriate measures to fix the problem by saving both money and resources. By influencing your immediate community, you are helping your locality, but collectively we can take on the world's most important problem, one meal at a time.
