Food Waste Trends

Data Analysis

Dover-Sherborn High School's Foodnomics chapter, co-led by Wenyao He and Wenqi Ding, has achieved exceptional results through their focused approach on lunch period waste reduction. Their systematic tracking and peer education efforts have produced remarkable improvements over the academic year.

Starting in September 2024 with an average lunch waste of $348.63, the team implemented consistent data collection and awareness campaigns using the Foodnomics app. Their collaborative leadership approach has created a strong culture of environmental responsibility among students.

Outstanding achievements at Dover-Sherborn High School include:

  1. Dramatic overall reduction: 51.2% total waste reduction from September to May
  2. Consistent month-over-month improvements: Achieved reductions in 8 out of 9 months tracked
  3. Significant cost savings: Reduced from $348.63 to $170.08 average monthly lunch waste
  4. Accelerated improvement: Strongest reductions in final three months (11.8%, 12.6%)
  5. Dual leadership success: Collaborative approach between Wenyao He and Wenqi Ding

The Dover-Sherborn chapter demonstrates how focused efforts on a single meal period, combined with strong collaborative leadership, can yield outstanding environmental and economic benefits. Their sustained improvements show the power of consistent peer education and the effectiveness of the Foodnomics tracking methodology.

Foodnomics

At Foodnomics, we recognize a long-standing problem as poverty and hunger, and we are determined to address it with the next generation of the world's leaders. The world produces more than enough food to feed its people, yet millions of people are still hungry, struggling to find their next meal. A contributing factor to this problem is waste. All across America, 60 million tons of food are being discarded each year, This is especially prevalent in dining halls across the country. But together, we can lessen this problem. By founding a chapter at your school and using the resources that we provide, you can collect data on how much food is being wasted and its monetary value, then work with your dining hall staff to take appropriate measures to fix the problem by saving both money and resources. By influencing your immediate community, you are helping your locality, but collectively we can take on the world's most important problem, one meal at a time.
