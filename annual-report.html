Data Collection Methodology

In the fall, I made a data collection template that I shared to all of our chapter leaders (which you can see by navigating to the "Chapters" section of the website). Using the Foodnomics app, we tracked the total food waste cost of four meals every month, aiming for twice near the beginning of the month and two times at the end of the month, from September to May. In between these times, our chapter members worked through their existing training to raise awareness around the issue of food waste, including encouraging people to download the Foodnomics app to track their own waste and aim to reduce it day after day. Immediately, we saw results.

Detailed Performance Metrics

Over time, from September, 10 Foodnomics chapters reported an aggregate daily reduction of $504.75 in food waste costs since early September, representing a 12.88% decline across three months. This trend continued into the winter, where aggregate food waste costs dropped another 11.10% since December. Finally, in the Spring, our efforts experienced the most success, dropping total food waste costs by another 20.78% since the end of winter.

What We Learned

"The biggest takeaway we've learned throughout the year, looking at our data, is clear. It is actually very easy to reduce food waste for most people."

Those you are eating with at any given day are very conscientious about how much food they waste once they get used to it. We encourage you to try out our for yourself weekly routines to reduce your food waste, and let the Foodnomics app assist you on your journey!

Foodnomics

At Foodnomics, we recognize a long-standing problem as poverty and hunger, and we are determined to address it with the next generation of the world's leaders. The world produces more than enough food to feed its people, yet millions of people are still hungry, struggling to find their next meal. A contributing factor to this problem is waste. All across America, 60 million tons of food are being discarded each year, This is especially prevalent in dining halls across the country. But together, we can lessen this problem. By founding a chapter at your school and using the resources that we provide, you can collect data on how much food is being wasted and its monetary value, then work with your dining hall staff to take appropriate measures to fix the problem by saving both money and resources. By influencing your immediate community, you are helping your locality, but collectively we can take on the world's most important problem, one meal at a time.
